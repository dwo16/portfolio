STREET SPEAK

Overview:

This project was part of a design assignment in my Human-Computer Interaction Class, to create an app that serves an underserved student population. I chose to focus on international exchange students who face challenges understanding casual, slang-heavy English used in everyday campus life.

Inspiration:

The idea came from sitting next to a foreign exchange student from China who often struggled to follow conversations filled with idioms and non-literal phrases. While classroom English was manageable, informal speech — the kind filled with slang, abbreviations, and cultural references — made social integration much harder.

Login / Sign Up / Start Pages

Simple Buttons and Text Prompts

Splash and Learning Center Pages

A fixed panel displays key metrics like Weekly Learning Progress and Slang Word of the Day for quick reference and motivation.

Scrollable content showcases videos, translations, and learning tools. Users can explore lessons without losing progress visibility.

A persistent bar with clear icons provides easy access to main features like the Learning Center, Translation, and Networking, ensuring consistent navigation for all users.

Flashcard Learning pages

Displays the lesson title and progress (e.g., 2/20) to keep users oriented and motivated during study sessions.
Interactive flashcard with a “Tap to Flip” cue encourages engagement and supports active recall learning. The speaker icon allows users to hear pronunciation for better language comprehension.

Dictionary Pages

The dictionary home provides clear entry points for each slang category, promoting quick navigation and discovery. Tabs for Social, Internet, and Student slang allow users to switch seamlessly between language contexts without reloading screens. Simple typography and clean spacing make slang definitions easy to scan and understand, especially for non-native speakers. Highlight colors and subtle dividers improve readability while keeping the interface approachable and consistent.

Translation Pages

Each translation mode—Camera, Text, Voice, and Conversation—uses a clean, task-focused layout to reduce confusion for new users. Large input areas and clear action cues (“Hold the Microphone,” “Enter your text”) emphasize accessibility and ease of use. Real-time feedback through “Translating...” messages keeps users informed, while the minimal interface ensures focus on the translation task.

Social Media Pages

The networking section helps users find peers with similar backgrounds, encouraging cultural exchange and community building. Profile cards and messaging buttons are visually distinct to simplify interaction and reduce navigation steps. The chat layout uses familiar messaging patterns with clear color contrast for readability, promoting comfort and natural conversation flow for non-native speakers.

Overall Wireframe
